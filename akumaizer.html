Akumaizer 3 - Main Page

If there’s one thing that Ishinomori never lacked, it was unique character designs and ideas for monsters. The main protagonist is usually the most thought-out and well formulated design, and most importantly, they need to have a human form portrayed by an actor. Behind every Kamen Rider, there’s a Hongo Takeshi, a genius cyclist popular with the ladies. For every Akarenger, there’s a Tsuyoshi Taijo, a brilliant strategist and handsome face.

The opposite logic is applied to monsters. They usually have no human counterpart that they transform from, and most of the time they’re not designed to be likable or friendly in the slightest. They need to be gruesome and grotesque, and kick puppies for fun. This design philosophy can get overwhelming however, as during a 30-50 episode series, the artist needs to design a multitude of creatures, especially operating with the “monster of the week” principle, where they’re usually defeated and explode by the end of the 20 minute episode.

Akumaizer 3 takes this principle and flips it on its head. There is no sole main protagonist, rather, there are three of them. Here, there are no handsome faces or perfect personalities. There’s no human counterparts, either. The main characters of Akumaizer 3 – are the monsters. The main protagonists are all played by suit actors, with dubbing by famous actors overlaid on top of their performances.
