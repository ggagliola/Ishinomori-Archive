Kaiketsu Zubat - Main Page

The “Golden Age” of tokusatsu to many fans of the genre harkens back to older shows that aired during the 1950s, which were much more down to earth and mellow compared to the series that would come after it. While they were still creative and flashy for what they were, they were still treated as actual television series with competent writing and character arcs that greatly affected the plot. Black and white series like Golden Bat, Might Atom, and Moonlight Mask are fondly remembered for this, but the expansion of the genre into wacky and downright absurd premises turned away many fans of the genre.

To solve this issue, Ishinmori would create Kaiketsu Zubat. While it still focuses on a larger than life hero who transforms to fight his opponents, it has a much more grounded and generally calmer feeling than the comically justice-driven heroes that aired alongside him. The series is also unique for the main designs of nearly every character. Villains are instead criminals named Bouncers, who take a more classical gangster wardrobe in their design. Many of the show’s settings are across smaller towns away from larger cities, and rural areas where police are usually slow to respond, if at all, so this adds even more to the “American western” style of cinema that Japan loved at the time. This series takes everything about the 70’s era of tokusatsu and flips it onto its head, while maintaining the joyous and retro feel of the 1950’s.
