Robot Detective - Story

The series opens up with a grizzly scene. A murder has just taken place, with no clear motives or incentives. The most disturbing part? There’s no clear evidence or any detectable traces of someone entering or leaving the room. The Special Forensic Investigation Office is stumped entirely, much to the frustration of Daizo Shiba, a 25 year veteran of the force. It’s not until he’s assigned a new partner that a breakthrough in the case is made. The short-tempered detective is frustrated to learn however, that his new partner is a robot named K, only seeing him as a useless metal doll. K however, is the breakthrough the team needs. Thanks to his dead-calm demeanor, and emotionless insight into the possibilities, he’s able to deduce exactly what happened, and trace it back to a monster created by the aptly named BAD Criminal Syndicate, a terroristic group who rent out monsters to criminals, and help track down the monster and ultimately destroy it, bringing it to justice.

Shiba struggles at home however, as ever since his wife was killed, he’s been raising his two daughters alone. After bringing K back home with him however, his two daughters are enamored by him, again, much to his dismay. As the series progresses onward, K learns more about what it means to be human, as he learns about the value of family and friendship, as well as more menial activities like drawing and writing poetry. By the end of the series, Shiba eventually learns to respect K as if he were his own blood. When BAD is finally dismantled and brought to justice, Shiba fully accepts K, and tells him that they should work together as partners forever.
