Legacy

Kikaider is fondly remembered by both casual audiences and fans of Ishinomori’s work. The show was also a part of an initiative by Toei to appeal to American audiences, as Kikaider 01 would air subtitled in the Hawaiian islands, quickly becoming an icon for Japanese-Americans living there as a symbol for their heritage.

Kikaider has also had a fair share of reboots and reimaginings of the iconic series. A darker reimagining of the series’ main antagonist would appear in 1995’s Mechanical Violator Hakaider. This reimagining of the series’ villain sees Hakaider as an antihero working alongside guerilla rebels to liberate the city of Jesus Town from the regime of Gurjev, a cruel dictator who lobotomizes anyone who contradicts his word, and his android guardian Michael, an all white yes-man who much to the dismay of fans, was revealed to be a reprogrammed version of the usual hero, Kikaider. In 2000 and 2003, an anime remake of the original series Android Kikaider: The Animation and Kikaider 01: The Animation aired in Japan as well as the United States under Adult Swim’s Toonami block. The series pushes the limitations the live-action format had with extended fight scenes and a much darker atmosphere to focus on the conflicting nature of Kikaider himself.
